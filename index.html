About Me

I'm Bota Duisenbay, an AI and Robotics Engineer with hands-on experience in both academic research and industry. My current work spans UAV navigation, mapping and computer vision.

I hold a Master’s degree in Artificial Intelligence and Robotics from Sapienza University of Rome and a Bachelor’s in Robotics and Mechatronics from Nazarbayev University.

At CNR, I currently lead open-field drone missions for precision agriculture—developing path planning strategies that combine real sensor data with information gain metrics. Previously, as an AI Engineer at Botshelf.ai, I built document intelligence systems by combining OCR, layout analysis, and LLMs for structured data extraction. At Nazarbayev University, I worked on robotic manipulation using the UR10 arm, integrating ROS and computer vision for object grasping tasks.

Whether enabling drones to navigate tomato fields or training end-to-end pipelines for document parsing, I’m driven by the challenge of turning abstract ideas into functioning systems. I enjoy solving problems at the intersection of AI, robotics, and machine learning—with a strong focus on practical deployment.
